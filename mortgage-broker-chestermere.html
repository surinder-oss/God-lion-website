What Makes a Chestermere Mortgage Different

Chestermere isn't a standard Calgary suburb — it's a lake community, with waterfront homes, brand-new subdivisions, and acreages on the edges. That mix changes how a mortgage gets approved, and it's exactly where working with a broker pays off.

Lakeside homes are bigger mortgages — and that can work in your favour

A lot of Chestermere homes sit at price points where buyers put 20% or more down, which makes the mortgage "uninsured." That sounds like a drawback, but it actually opens the door to a wider set of lenders and often competitive uninsured rates. The trick is structuring your down payment and application to take advantage of it — something we map out with you before you make an offer, not after.

Acreages, waterfront, and rural properties near the lake

Not every property around Chestermere is a standard city lot. Acreages, homes on well and septic, and waterfront lots don't get financed the same way as a downtown condo — some lenders cap how much land they'll count toward value, and others avoid rural-residential files altogether. We know which lenders are comfortable with these properties and place your mortgage where it will actually fund.

Three paths to approval, not one

There isn't one way to get a mortgage — there are three, and the right one depends on your file. A-lenders (the banks and credit unions) offer the most competitive rates when you have strong credit and provable income. B-lenders are built for self-employed, bruised-credit, or unusual-income files, usually as a one- to two-year bridge back to an A-lender. Private lenders are equity-focused for tight timelines or complex files, and generally need 20–25% or more down. A single bank can only offer you the first path — we shop all three and build a plan to move you up to better terms over time.

Chestermere Mortgage Questions, Answered

Do I need to live in Chestermere to work with you?

No. We're licensed across Alberta and work with buyers, owners, and renewers throughout Chestermere and the surrounding area. Everything is handled by phone, email, and secure e-signing, so you never have to visit an office.

Can I get a mortgage in Chestermere if I'm self-employed?

Yes — it's one of our specialties, and Chestermere has a lot of business owners. A-lenders usually average your last two years of declared income, while alternative (B) lenders can often work from about a year of business bank statements instead, which helps if you write off heavily at tax time. We match your file to the lender that reads it best.

How much down payment do I need for a Chestermere home?

For an owner-occupied home, as little as 5% on the first $500,000 and 10% on the portion above that, up to a $1.5 million purchase price. Many higher-end lakeside homes are bought with 20% or more down, which makes the mortgage uninsured and opens access to a wider set of lenders. We'll give you the exact number for your plan before you shop.

Can you finance an acreage or waterfront property near Chestermere?

Yes. Acreages, homes on well and septic, and waterfront lots aren't financed like a standard city lot — some lenders limit how much land they'll value or avoid rural-residential files altogether. We work with lenders who are comfortable with these properties and place your mortgage where it will actually fund.

Do I need a stress test to renew my mortgage in Chestermere?

If you renew with your current lender, no stress test is required. Since late 2024 you can also switch to a new lender on a straight renewal without re-passing the stress test, which opens up the whole market. The renewal letter your bank sends is a starting point, not their best offer — a quick review often saves real money.
