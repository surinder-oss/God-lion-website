Buying or Renewing
a Mortgage in Regina

A practical look at how the mortgage process works in an affordable capital city with steady demand.

A market where qualifying is the real hurdle

Regina prices are gentle next to the big cities, so finding a home you can afford is rarely the hard part — the mortgage rules are. The federal stress test still asks you to prove you could handle payments at a higher rate than you'll actually pay, and small differences in how your income is documented can decide whether a lender says yes. Our job is to put your file in front of the lender most likely to approve it, and to tell you honestly where you stand before you make an offer.

New to Canada? There's a path for you

Regina welcomes new arrivals every year, and many want to buy before they've built a long Canadian credit history. The banks don't always make that easy. Several lenders run newcomer programs that look at your broader picture — steady income, savings, and rent history — instead of only a Canadian credit score. If you've been told you need years of history before you can buy, it's worth a second opinion. We do this often.

Self-employed and small-business owners

Alongside its public-sector jobs, Regina has plenty of business owners and trades, and if you write off a healthy portion of your income for tax purposes, a big bank's rigid checklist can work against you. This is where an independent broker earns their place. We work with lenders who understand business-for-self income and can consider bank statements or stated-income programs alongside your notices of assessment. The goal is to show your real earning power in a way a lender can say yes to, rather than forcing your file into a box it was never going to fit.

Renewals and refinances in Regina

When your mortgage comes up for renewal, the offer your current lender mails out is rarely their sharpest. A lot of owners simply sign it because it's easy. Shopping the wider market at renewal is one of the most dependable ways to compare what's actually available to you. The same applies to refinancing, whether you're consolidating higher-interest debt, funding a renovation, or accessing equity you've built up over the years. We lay out the options, the costs, and the trade-offs in plain language so you can decide with confidence.

What to gather before you apply

Getting organised early keeps your file moving. For most applications, lenders want recent pay stubs or, for business owners, two years of notices of assessment and financial statements, plus identification, a record of your down payment source, and details of any existing debts. We send a clear checklist up front so nothing slows you down at the offer stage. When you're ready, a short conversation is usually enough for us to point you toward the right next step.
