What Makes a Lethbridge Mortgage Different

Lethbridge doesn't move like Calgary or Edmonton. Prices are lower, the pace is calmer, and a lot of people here earn a living in ways the big banks aren't built to read. That's where a broker earns their keep.

An affordable market — if the financing works

Lethbridge is consistently one of the more affordable cities in Alberta to own a home, which puts ownership within reach for many first-time buyers and young families. The catch is that affordability on paper doesn't matter if your file gets declined. Getting the financing right — the down payment, the qualifying income, the right lender — is what turns a good price into a set of keys. A real pre-approval before you shop tells you exactly what you can offer, and it can hold a rate for up to 120 days while you look.

Income the banks don't always understand

Lethbridge runs on agriculture, small business, the University of Lethbridge and the college, healthcare, and the trades. That means a lot of people earn farm income, self-employment income, contract income, or a mix — and write plenty off at tax time. That's smart business, but it can make a bank think you earn less than you really do. Alternative (B) lenders can often work from roughly your last twelve months of business bank statements instead of your tax returns, and they read the whole story rather than a single number. Knowing which lenders do this well is most of the job.

Three paths to approval, not one

There isn't one way to get a mortgage — there are three, and the right one depends on your file. A-lenders (the banks and credit unions) offer the most competitive rates if you have strong credit and provable income. B-lenders are built for self-employed, bruised-credit, or unusual-income files, usually as a one- to two-year bridge back to an A-lender. Private lenders are equity-focused for tight timelines or complex files, and generally need 20–25% or more down. A single bank can only offer you the first path. We shop all three and build the plan to move you up to better terms over time.

Lethbridge Mortgage Questions, Answered

Do I need to live in Lethbridge to work with you?

No. We're licensed across Alberta and work with buyers, owners, and renewers throughout Lethbridge and the surrounding communities. Everything is handled by phone, email, and secure e-signing, so you never have to visit an office.

Can I get a mortgage in Lethbridge if I'm self-employed?

Yes — self-employed files are one of our specialties. A-lenders usually average your last two years of declared income, while alternative (B) lenders can often use about a year of business bank statements instead, which helps if you write off heavily. The right approach depends on your file, and sorting that out is exactly what we do.

What credit score do I need to buy a home in Lethbridge?

There's no single cutoff. A-lenders want stronger credit, but B-lenders regularly work with scores below 600 when the rest of the file is solid, and private lenders look mainly at your equity and down payment. A past bankruptcy, consumer proposal, or collection doesn't automatically disqualify you.

How much down payment do I need?

For an owner-occupied home, as little as 5% on the first $500,000 of the purchase price. A rental or investment property generally needs at least 20% down, and private financing usually asks for 20–25% or more. We'll give you the real number for your specific plan before you start shopping.

Should I just renew with my current lender?

Not without checking your options first. The renewal letter your lender sends is a starting point, not their best offer — and since late 2024 you can switch lenders on a straight renewal without re-passing the stress test, which opens up the whole market. A quick review often saves real money.
