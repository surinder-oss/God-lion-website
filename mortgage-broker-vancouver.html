Buying or Renewing
a Mortgage in Vancouver

A practical look at how the mortgage process works in one of the country's most expensive markets.

A market where qualifying is the real hurdle

In Vancouver, finding a place you like is rarely the hard part — qualifying for the mortgage is. High prices mean bigger loans, and the federal stress test asks you to prove you could handle payments at a rate higher than the one you'll actually pay. Small differences in how your income is documented can decide whether a lender says yes. Our job is to bring your file to the lender most likely to approve it, and to tell you honestly where you stand before you make an offer.

New to Canada? There's a path for you

Vancouver is one of the first places many newcomers to Canada settle, and a bank won't always make it easy when your credit history here is still thin. Several lenders run newcomer programs that look at your whole picture — steady income, savings, and rent history — instead of only a Canadian credit score. If you've been told you need years of history before you can buy, it's worth a second opinion. We do this often.

Self-employed and small-business owners

Vancouver has no shortage of business owners and contractors, and if you write off a good chunk of your income at tax time, a bank's rigid checklist can work against you. This is where an independent broker earns their place. We work with lenders who understand business-for-self income and can look at bank statements or stated-income programs alongside your notices of assessment. The goal is to show your real earning power in a way a lender can say yes to, instead of forcing your file into a box it was never going to fit.

Renewals and refinances in Vancouver

If your mortgage is coming up for renewal, the offer your current lender mails out is rarely their best. A lot of homeowners sign it just because it's easy. Shopping the wider market at renewal is one of the most reliable ways to see what's really available to you. The same goes for refinancing, whether you're rolling in higher-interest debt, paying for a renovation, or tapping equity that's grown as Vancouver values have climbed. We lay out the options, the costs, and the trade-offs in plain language so you can decide with confidence.

What to gather before you apply

Getting organised early keeps your file moving. For most applications, lenders want recent pay stubs or, for business owners, two years of notices of assessment and financial statements, plus identification, a record of where your down payment is coming from, and details of any existing debts. We send a clear checklist up front so nothing slows you down at the offer stage. When you're ready, a short conversation is usually enough for us to point you toward the right next step.
