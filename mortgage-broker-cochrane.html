What Makes a Cochrane Mortgage Different

Cochrane has grown fast as families look for a foothills lifestyle within commuting distance of Calgary. That means a mix of new subdivisions, established homes, and acreages on the edge of town — and each of those finances a little differently.

Acreages and foothills properties

A lot of what makes Cochrane appealing — space, acreages, homes on well and septic just outside town — doesn’t finance like a standard city lot. Some lenders cap how much land they’ll value or avoid rural residential altogether. We know which lenders are comfortable with acreage and foothills properties and place your mortgage where it will actually fund.

Commuter budgets and bigger mortgages

Many Cochrane buyers are moving up from Calgary and carrying a larger mortgage to get the home and the lifestyle. When you’re putting 20% or more down, your mortgage is uninsured — which sounds like a downside but actually opens access to a wider set of lenders. Structuring your down payment and file to take advantage of that is something we map out before you make an offer.

Three paths to approval, not one

There isn’t one way to get a mortgage — there are three, and the right one depends on your file. A-lenders (the banks and credit unions) offer the most competitive rates when you have strong credit and provable income. B-lenders are built for self-employed, bruised-credit, or unusual-income files, usually as a one- to two-year bridge back to an A-lender. Private lenders are equity-focused for tight timelines or complex files, and generally need 20–25% or more down. A single bank can only offer you the first path — we shop all three and build a plan to move you up to better terms over time.

Cochrane Mortgage Questions, Answered

Do I need to live in Cochrane to work with you?

No. We’re licensed across Alberta and work with buyers, owners, and renewers throughout Cochrane and the surrounding area. Everything is handled by phone, email, and secure e-signing, so you never have to visit an office.

Can you finance an acreage or rural property near Cochrane?

Yes. Acreages, homes on well and septic, and foothills properties aren’t financed like a standard city lot — some lenders limit how much land they’ll value or avoid rural residential. We work with lenders who are comfortable with these properties and place your file where it will fund.

How much down payment do I need?

For an owner-occupied home, as little as 5% on the first $500,000 and 10% on the portion above that, up to a $1.5 million purchase price. A rental or investment property generally needs at least 20% down, and private financing usually asks for 20–25% or more. We’ll give you the real number for your plan before you start shopping.

What credit score do I need to buy a home in Cochrane?

There’s no single cutoff. A-lenders want stronger credit, but B-lenders regularly work with scores below 600 when the rest of the file is solid, and private lenders look mainly at your equity and down payment. A past bankruptcy, consumer proposal, or collection doesn’t automatically disqualify you.

Should I just renew with my current lender?

Not without checking your options first. The renewal letter your lender sends is a starting point, not their best offer — and since late 2024 you can switch lenders on a straight renewal without re-passing the stress test, which opens up the whole market. A quick review often saves real money.
